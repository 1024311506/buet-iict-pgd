About IICT

The Institute of Information and Communication Technology (IICT) is one of the institutes of the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). The Syndicate of BUET decided on 28th June 2001 to establish the institute, and its formal inauguration was held on 2nd September 2001.

This decision was made following a government directive to produce more professionals in the IT sector. The former BUET Computer Center served as the foundation for IICT, and was reorganized to support the expanded objectives of the new institute.

The main objective of the Institute is to offer postgraduate programs, in addition to continuing the services previously provided by the Computer Center.

Location

IICT is situated on the sixth and seventh floors of the ECE Building at BUET. Within BUET's calm academic environment, IICT offers an ideal setting for technology-based education. It is easily accessible from all parts of the city and is close to residential areas, markets, universities, industries, and research centers.

Aims and Objectives of IICT

The purpose of the Institute is to provide a platform for teaching, learning, and research in Information and Communication Technology (ICT), which contributes to industrial and infrastructural development, economic growth, and national prosperity. The aims and objectives include:

  • To actively pursue advanced research in ICT in order to develop knowledge-based products and services;
  • To develop ICT facilities and foster an on-campus environment that encourages innovation, enterprise, and the incubation of promising ideas and initiatives;
  • To produce skilled professionals in ICT through training programs for newcomers as well as for those already in the profession;
  • To provide consulting and advisory services to public and private sector organizations.
